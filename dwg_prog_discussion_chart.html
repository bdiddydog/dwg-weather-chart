PATTERN EVOLUTION · WEEK 2 CHANGES

8-14 DAY
↖️
ALEUTIAN RIDGE RETROGRADING
Strong 500-hPa ridge west of Alaska retrogresses somewhat and slowly weakens. Subnormal 500-hPa heights encompass entire state. Largest uncertainty in the forecast.
ECENS preferred as compromise solution
➡️
W. CONUS RIDGE EXPANDING
Strong mid-level ridge covering the western CONUS drifts eastward and expands northward. Models in good agreement on this evolution. Ridge reaches into central CONUS.
Above-normal temps reach Lower Mississippi Valley
⬆️
EASTERN TROUGH LIFTING
Mid-level trough over eastern CONUS begins to gradually deamplify and lift to the north-northeast. GEFS/CNENS keep more amplitude; ECENS quicker to retract northward. Amplified solutions favored.
Trough axis: key uncertainty for E. CONUS precip
🌺
HAWAII TROUGH WEAKENING
The strong negative 500-hPa height anomaly NW of Hawaii lifts northwestward away from the state and progressively weakens over the course of week 2. Precipitation decreases in coverage and intensity but remains above normal.
>50% above-normal precip statewide
🔥
WARM ANOMALY EXPANDING EAST
Mid-level ridge in the West drives enhanced above-normal temperature chances reaching as far southeast as the Lower Mississippi Valley and as far north as the Canadian border.
>70% above-normal: Four Corners / Great Basin / CA
🌨️
NE COLD ANOMALY PERSISTING
Subnormal temperatures forecast in association with mid-level trough moving through the eastern CONUS. Coldest conditions over central and western Northeast where negative 500-hPa heights are most persistent.
>60% below-normal temps: central/western NE
