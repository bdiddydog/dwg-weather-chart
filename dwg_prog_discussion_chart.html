Synoptic Pattern Overview

Mid-level ridge dominates Alaska with axis near Alaska-Yukon border. Weak trough approaches southwestern CONUS while deeper troughing persists over eastern regions. Positive 500-hPa height anomalies extend from northern Mexico through Gulf into Florida. This pattern drives below-normal temperatures across Plains, Mississippi Valley, Great Lakes, Mid-Atlantic, and Northeast, with probabilities exceeding 50% in Upper Mississippi Valley. Enhanced precipitation expected along frontal boundary affecting Southeast, Mid-Atlantic coast, and Northeast.

Synoptic Pattern Evolution

Tools show better agreement on stronger trough-ridge dipole over Alaska/northwest Canada. Blocking ridge persists over southern Greenland while northeastern CONUS troughing continues. Weak ridge development over western CONUS with generally zonal flow pattern emerging. This creates challenging temperature and precipitation forecasting conditions. Below-normal temperatures persist east of Rockies (excluding Rio Grande Valley, Gulf Coast, Florida) with probabilities exceeding 50% over Great Lakes, Ohio Valley, and Northeast.
